Sinigang Recipe

Sinigang is a very famous food originated in the Philippines. It is a stew or soup that is sour.>

Other country also have other food similar to our Sinigang like Vietnam's Cahn Chua. It is also a stew and also uses tamarind to make the soup sour.

Vietnam's Cahn Chua also uses pineapple to make the soup sour aside from tamarind. It also uses more fish than pork.

Sinigang on the other hand, uses tamarind and tomatoes to make the soup sour. The recipes in cooking Sinigan uses meat and fish.

Sinigang na Baboy

This type or version of sinigang is one of the most known sinigang versions. It is just like the other sinigang but it uses pork combined with different vegetables like okra, radish and eggplant.

Sinigang na Bangus

This sinigang is like the first one but it uses fish. I think this version is much faster to cook since fish are more faster to cook than the pork.

Sinigang na Manok

Sinigang na Manok is just like the Sinigang na Baboy. You will just change the meat that you will be using. The ingredients for Sinigang na Baboy and Sinigang na Manok are pretty much the same. They will differ in terms of time of cooking because it takes time to make the pork soft.

Sinigang na Tilapia

Also like the other sinigang mentioned earlier, this version is pretty much the same except that this recipe uses ginger. Other vegetables are optional.

Sinigang na Lapu-Lapu

It is also just like the other sinigang but this recipe uses lapu-lapu.

Sinigang sa Miso

This version uses fish, commonly salmon heads. Fermented soybean with barley or miso is added in the soup so it is called sinigang sa miso.

Sinigang na Salmon Belly

This recipe uses salmon belly but the procedures and ingredients are pretty much the same. This recipe is mostly in a restaurants's menu.

Sinigang na Hipon

Again, this version is like the others. It is also one of the famous dishes in a filipino restaurant.

Sinigang na Manok

Sinigang na Manok is commonly cooked by the Filipinos because of its taste and it is easy to cook.

Course Type: Main Course

Cuisine: Filipino

Keyword: Sinigang, Sinigang na Manok

Prep Time: 10 mins

Cook Time: 45 mins

Total Time: 55 mins

Serving: 4 people

Calories: 222 calories per serving

Author: Clarenz Joy L. Aumentado

Ingredients

  • Tamarind or tamarind powder
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Kangkong
  • Green bean
  • Radish
  • Okra
  • Finger chili
  • Chicken

    • Instructions

  1. Prepare the ingredients.
  2. Boil the chicken in a caserole together with onions, tomatoes, finger chili and season it with salt.
  3. When the chicken is already cooked, put the tamarind powder then adjust the taste according to your liking.
  4. Put the vegetables then cook it for about 5-15 minutes.
  5. Serve it with rice.
